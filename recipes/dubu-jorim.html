Dubu Jorim

Back

Description

Think tofu is boring? Then you need to try fiery, garlicky, dubu jorim, a Korean braised tofu that will have you reaching for more bowls of rice and also serves as a great accompaniment to an ice cold beer.

Since it requires only tofu and pantry staples, dubu jorim is about the cheapest and easiest meal you can make after instant ramen or a PB&J. Full of protein and flavor, it's great as a midnight snack or a quick weeknight side.

Main Ingredients (serves 1 or 2 if you're nice)

  • 1 pack firm tofu, sliced into 1/4" thick squares
  • 1 green onion sliced, some reserved for garnish
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • Few spoons oil for cooking
  • Toasted sesame oil and sesame seeds for garnish

Ingredients for sauce:

  • Soy sauce
  • Sugar
  • Chili flakes
  • Water
  • Sesame oil

Steps

  1. Heat oil in large frypan on high heat
  2. Working in batches if necessary, fry tofu slices until golden
  3. Remove and add onions, garlic, and green onion
  4. Add sauce
  5. Once sauce has reduced somewhat and onions are soft, return tofu to pan
  6. Mix well and simmer 5 minutes
  7. Serve garnished with more green onion, sesame seeds, and sesame oil. Bowl of hot white rice required.
